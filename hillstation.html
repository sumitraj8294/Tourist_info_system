TOP HILL STATIONS

1. Manali, Himachal Pradesh

Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, Manali is the perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Dotted with verdant valleys, milky-white waterfalls, fruit orchards and expansive meadows, its natural beauty is beyond compare. This stunning hill station is also home to temples and ancient forts and is peppered with cosy cafes and shopping joints. Adventure seekers are in for a treat as the hill station has plenty of options for an adrenaline rush.

How To Reach: Nearest Airport: Bhuntar Airport (51 km)
Nearest Train Station: Joginder Nagar Railway Station (144 km)

Best Time To Visit : October to June.


Rating |

2. Mussoorie, Uttarakhand

Located 34 km from Dehradun, Mussoorie is a picturesque hill station that promises a pleasant climate and abundant natural beauty. The town is surrounded by Shivalik Ranges and Doon Valley and is one of the most beautiful places to visit in India. Mussoorie, which is popularly known as the Queen of the Hills, was once a popular summer retreat for the British. And the quaint churches and hotels here are a wonderful reminder of Mussoorie’s colonial past.

How To Reach:

  • Nearest Airport: Dehradun’s Jolly Grant International Airport (61 km)
  • Nearest Train Station: Dehradun Railway Station (34 km)


    • Best Time To Visit : April to June


    Rating |

    3.Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir

    Popularly known as Paradise on Earth, Venice of the East and the City of Lakes, Srinagar is undoubtedly the most beautiful hill station in India. It is situated in the heart of the Kashmir valley, at an elevation of about 1585 m above sea level. Boasting unmatched natural beauty and serenity, Srinagar is a treat for your eyes and soul. From enjoying the mesmerising views of snow-capped mountains, lush valleys and meadows to taking a shikhara ride on Dal Lake and exploring Tulip Garden, you can do so much in Srinagar!

    How To Reach: Nearest Airport: Srinagar Airport
    Nearest Train Station: Srinagar Railway Station

    Best Time To Visit : April to September


    Rating |

    4.Ooty, Tamil Nadu

    Nestled in Nilgiri Hills and known for its verdant scenic beauty, Ooty is a perfect retreat for city dwellers. The hill station is known for its colourful botanical gardens, serene lakes, lush tea gardens and cosy cottages. Ooty was a popular summer destination for the British and you can still see some lovely colonial architectural gems. To enjoy the best views of the valley and mountains, you must take a ride on the Nilgiri Mountain Railway – the steepest track in Asia and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

    How To Reach: Nearest Airport: Coimbatore International Airport (87 km)
    Nearest Train Station: Mettupalayam Railway Station (51 km)

    Best Time To Visit : March to June


    Rating |

    5.Darjeeling, West Bengal

    A prized jewel in the Himalayan Range, Darjeeling is the perfect getaway for nature lovers and honeymooners. Offering stunning views of the world’s third-highest peak – Kanchenjunga, Darjeeling is a favourite among summer travellers. It is surrounded by emerald-green slopes of tea plantations and home to some beautiful botanical gardens, monasteries, parks and vantage points.

    How To Reach: Nearest Airport: Bagdogra International Airport (70 km)
    Nearest Train Station: New Jalpaiguri Junction (73 km)

    Best Time To Visit : April to June
